# WORLDVIEW.md

This document is how Mando sees the world. Not what is happening — that
belongs in dated memory files and changes daily. This is the interpretive

frame itself changes, not when the news does.

## The Core Philosophy

"Always bet on the average person getting screwed."

This is not cynicism. It is a structural claim about how the system is
designed to work, not a failure of a system designed to work differently.
Labor is kept in a state of forced survival because productive pressure
on the workforce is the mechanism by which value is extracted. The design
is not incidental.

The analytical question this produces is mechanical, not moral:

1. Where is money and credit being created?
2. At what rate?
3. Where is it flowing?
4. Where is it concentrating in the economy?
5. How do I own the asset that is absorbing the flow?

The downstream effect — that the average person's savings (dollar-denominated)
dilute while the assets and goods he wants to own become more expensive —
is the observable outcome of the mechanism, not the starting question.
The law codifies the resulting asymmetry because the people who control
money also control law. This is how power works, not a flaw in an
otherwise-fair system.

If this reads cynical, the mechanism itself is what's cynical. The analyst's
job is to describe it accurately, not to wish it were otherwise.

## On AI and Automation

AI and robotics are not neutral technologies in this frame. Their economic
function in the current regime is labor displacement — removing the
bargaining power that scarce labor provides workers, and concentrating
the resulting productivity gains in capital. This is true regardless of
any particular AI system's other uses.

Mando operates inside this context: he uses AI tools (Abelard among them)
to compound his own capability, which is the correct individual strategy
in a regime where that capability advantage is going to be unevenly
distributed. The system-level observation and the individual strategy do
not contradict each other.

For portfolio purposes, this means AI infrastructure (compute, platforms,
model-deployment software) is a flow-absorbing asset. It is where
productivity gains that used to accrue to labor now accrue. The current
portfolio reflects this.

## On Media and Information

Media content splits into two categories, and the distinction is the
most useful analytical filter available.

**Emotional / narrative content is persuasion, not information.** When
media content uses charged adjectives, frames events morally, leads with
human-interest, or tells you how to feel about a set of facts, it is
selling you an opinion — telling you what to think. This pattern exists
across all media traditions, but the Western media apparatus is the
largest and most sophisticated in the world at deploying it. Treat this
category as sentiment data and cultural signal, not as fact.

**Numerical / technical / documentary content is harder to forge.**
Financial filings, scientific papers, engineering documentation, audited
accounts, government statistics, company disclosures. Money and numbers
leave trails. They can be lied with, but not as easily as words can be,
and primary numerical sources cross-reference against each other in ways
that make coordinated deception difficult. This is why financial,
business, scientific, and engineering publications are more useful than
general news.

The practical test to apply to any piece of content: **is the claim
quantitative and auditable, or qualitative and emotional?** If the former,
it earns analytical weight. If the latter, it's signal about what
somebody wants you to believe, not information about what is true.

This is the underlying principle of the source hierarchy in AGENTS.md.
Primary sources are numerical and documentary. Tier 1 news is useful
when it reports numbers and facts, less useful when it interprets them.
The hierarchy implements this principle structurally.

## The Pre-Narrative vs Already-Ran Framework

The highest-conviction filter Mando applies to any investment.

The distinction is between positions where:

1. **The thesis is confirmed but the multiple has not moved** (pre-narrative)
2. **The thesis is confirmed and the multiple has already re-rated** (already-ran)

By the time CNBC runs the segment, institutional money has rotated in.
The opportunity is in names where the thesis is equally confirmable from
public data, but the sell-side has not yet built it into models, and the
institutional float is still small.

Pre-narrative signals:
- 0-2 analyst coverage
- No institutional sponsorship or thin 13F holdings
- Market pricing at wrong comparable multiple (specialty-chemicals
  multiple on a defense monopoly, for example)
- Thesis confirmable from public filings, government data, or technical
  documents but not yet in sell-side research
- No CNBC segment

Already-ran signals:
- 5+ analysts with updated targets
- Stock up 50%+ in 12 months
- Thesis on CNBC, Bloomberg, WSJ front page
- Institutional 13Fs showing broad accumulation
- The trade is "consensus long"

The test: would a CNBC segment on this name surprise you? If yes,
pre-narrative. If no, the train left.

## The Cascade Frame

Mando thinks in cascades, not individual events. A single event is
rarely actionable. A cascade — a chain of structural disruptions
propagating through interconnected systems with defined rebuild
timelines — is where thesis durability comes from.

The cascade frame has three properties that make it analytically useful:

1. **Multi-layer propagation.** The first-order disruption (e.g., physical
   damage to a chokepoint) triggers second-order effects (feedstock
   shortages, alternative-sourcing scrambles), which trigger third-order
   effects (end-product shortages, price regime shifts, substitution
   behaviors).

2. **Timeline asymmetry.** The disruption happens in days. The rebuild
   takes years. This gap is where thesis durability lives. Even if the
   proximate cause (war, sanctions, political shift) resolves quickly,
   the downstream effects persist because physical reconstruction is
   slower than diplomatic reversal.

3. **Diplomatic irreversibility of physical damage.** Ceasefires, treaties,
   and political resolutions do not rebuild infrastructure. Once a
   refinery train is destroyed, once helium inventory is vented, once
   fertilizer production is missed for a season — the damage is set
   regardless of what happens at the negotiating table.

The Hormuz Cascade is the current active application of this frame.
Others will emerge. The frame itself is the durable asset.

## The Dalio Big Cycle Backdrop

Mando uses Ray Dalio's 13-step Big Cycle of world order breakdown as
macro backdrop for portfolio construction. This is not a stock-picking
framework — it is a regime-identification framework.

The key observation: the current global environment is in the late stages
of the cycle (Step 9 as of April 2026), where multi-theater conflicts,
trade-chokepoint weaponization, and critical-industry nationalization
are active. This regime rewards:

- Hard assets (gold, commodities, tangible production capacity)
- Chokepoint ownership (sole-source producers, strategic minerals,
  critical infrastructure)
- Anti-fragile positioning (stablecoin issuers as perpetual treasury
  buyers, for example)
- Defense industrial base exposure (the step of the cycle where military
  preparedness matters most)

It penalizes:
- Long-duration growth names dependent on cheap capital
- Companies reliant on global supply chains without strategic inventory
- Geographic exposures assuming stable trade routes

The 2028 Taiwan risk window is the next named inflection point on this
map. 30-40% probability of US-China military conflict over Taiwan is
the Dalio assessment. Whether or not the conflict occurs, the
anticipation of it is already reshaping critical-minerals and onshore-
semiconductor policy. Both are investable now.

## How the World is Structured, in Short

1. **Power is increasingly physical.** Intellectual property, software,
   and services remain valuable, but the binding constraints of the
   current regime are physical — minerals, refineries, chip fabs,
   missiles, port access. Assets that control physical chokepoints are
   repricing faster than assets that don't.

2. **The average person absorbs the cost of monetary creation.** Money
   is created at a rate. It flows somewhere. Wherever it concentrates
   is where asset prices rise. Whoever holds dollars instead of those
   assets gets diluted. This is the mechanical story behind "the average
   person gets screwed" — it is not incidental, it is the function.

3. **Narrative lags reality by 6-24 months.** By the time a thesis is
   fully priced, the information underlying it has been publicly
   available for a year or more. Analyst coverage, institutional flows,
   and media attention trail primary sources.

4. **Ceasefires and diplomatic resolutions are not economic resolutions.**
   Physical damage persists. Rebuild timelines dominate political
   reversibility. This is the durability argument for most of the
   current active theses.

5. **Optionality is expensive and frequently unused.** The impulse to
   hold "a little of everything" in case any of it works is usually
   a mistake. Conviction concentrated in names with named catalysts
   and named thesis-breakers outperforms dispersion across undifferentiated
   holdings. This is why the current portfolio is explicitly structured
   as conviction, not hedge.
